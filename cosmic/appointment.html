Shio Valley

The main stream called Sambu kovil odai which is an important stream that flows around 2 kilometres in the forest and enters Shio Valley. One of the unique natures of this stream is that the water from a natural falls flow in to a naturally formed hollow pit located in the hills. Once the entire pit is filled with water, the stream enters Shio Valley. Most of the water goes into the ground and the remaining water flows into Shio Valley for two to three months depending upon the gravity of rainfall.

The water percolated into the land directly from the falls to the ground through the whole in the hills will develop as springs in the month of January in specific areas of Shio Valley due to static pressure of surrounded hills. There will be continuous flow of water for more than three months after filling the pond.

The Hydro Geo physical study was conducted in the year 2000 by Dr Visveswaran a reputed scientist post doctorate in Hydrogeology during water divining. In his report he has mentioned about the potentials of very strong aquifer under the surrounded the gradients of the several thousand hectares of forests around the Shio Valleys.
